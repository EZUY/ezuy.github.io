Resources

Tools & resources I work with

A curated map of the data repositories, platforms, tools, and sequencing assays I use day to day in genomics and multi-omics research — with a brief note on each and a link to learn more.

Data repositories

GEO ↗

NCBI's Gene Expression Omnibus — the primary public archive for functional genomics data (microarray and sequencing), and where most published datasets are deposited and downloaded.

SRA ↗

The Sequence Read Archive stores raw sequencing reads behind GEO records. Pair it with the SRA Toolkit to pull FASTQ files for reanalysis.

UK Biobank ↗

A large-scale biomedical database with genetic and health data from half a million participants — a cornerstone resource for GWAS and complex-trait genetics.

GWAS Catalog ↗

A curated database of published genome-wide association studies and their trait-associated variants, maintained by EMBL-EBI and NHGRI.

Atlases & initiatives

CZ Biohub ↗

The Chan Zuckerberg Biohub network, developing open tools, atlases, and methods for single-cell biology and imaging — a hub for community datasets and software.

Human Cell Atlas ↗

A global effort to map every cell type in the human body at single-cell resolution — a rich reference for annotating and comparing your own single-cell data.

ENCODE ↗

The Encyclopedia of DNA Elements — genome-wide maps of regulatory regions, chromatin state, and transcription-factor binding across many cell types.

Workflows & reproducibility

Nextflow ↗

A workflow language that makes pipelines reproducible and portable — the same code runs on a laptop, an HPC cluster, or the cloud, with containers handling dependencies.

nf-core ↗

A community collection of production-ready Nextflow pipelines — RNA-seq, scRNA-seq, ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, and more — that are peer-reviewed, tested, and versioned.

Bioconductor ↗

The home of R packages for genomics — DESeq2, edgeR, limma, and hundreds more for bulk and single-cell analysis, with consistent data structures and documentation.

Single-cell & spatial

Seurat ↗

The standard R toolkit for single-cell analysis — QC, normalization, clustering, integration, and multimodal (WNN) workflows for scRNA-seq and spatial data.

Scanpy ↗

The Python counterpart to Seurat, built on AnnData — scalable single-cell preprocessing, clustering, and trajectory analysis that plays well with the scverse ecosystem.

10x Genomics ↗

Cell Ranger, Space Ranger, and Loupe Browser for processing and exploring Chromium single-cell and Visium spatial transcriptomics data.

scvi-tools ↗

Probabilistic deep-learning models (scVI and more) for single-cell data — batch correction, integration, and dimensionality reduction at scale.

Sequencing assays

RNA-seq ↗

Measures genome-wide gene expression. For differential expression and quantification I lean on the nf-core/rnaseq pipeline with DESeq2/edgeR downstream.

ChIP-seq ↗

Maps where proteins (transcription factors, histone marks) bind DNA. Peak calling and QC via the nf-core/chipseq pipeline.

ATAC-seq ↗

Profiles genome-wide chromatin accessibility to reveal active regulatory regions — analyzed with the nf-core/atacseq pipeline.

CUT&RUN ↗

A low-input alternative to ChIP-seq for mapping protein–DNA interactions with lower background — processed via the nf-core/cutandrun pipeline.

CRISPR screens ↗

Pooled functional screens to find genes driving a phenotype. I analyze read-count matrices for gene enrichment/depletion with MAGeCK.

Statistical genetics

PLINK ↗

The workhorse toolset for genome-wide association analysis and genotype data management — QC, association testing, and population-structure control.

LDSC ↗

LD Score Regression estimates SNP heritability and genetic correlation from GWAS summary statistics while accounting for linkage disequilibrium.

DESeq2 ↗

The go-to Bioconductor package for differential expression from bulk RNA-seq count data, with robust normalization and shrinkage estimation.
